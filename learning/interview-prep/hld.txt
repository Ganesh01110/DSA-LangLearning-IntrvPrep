Transitioning from Low-Level Design (LLD) to **High-Level Design (HLD)** shifts the focus from "How do I write clean code?" to "How do I scale this system to millions of users?"

In HLD, your tools aren't classes and patterns; they are databases, load balancers, messaging queues, and caching layers. Here is the tier-wise breakdown and the master prompt for your HLD preparation.

---

## Tier 1: The Beginner Level

**Focus:** The "Building Blocks" and the Request-Response cycle.

* **Core Concepts:**
* **Client-Server Architecture:** DNS, Load Balancers (Layer 4 vs. Layer 7).
* **Database Basics:** SQL vs. NoSQL (When to use which).
* **Horizontal vs. Vertical Scaling:** Why we prefer "scaling out."


* **Networking:** HTTP/HTTPS, REST APIs, and basic Status Codes.
* **Caching:** Why we need it and the concept of Content Delivery Networks (CDNs).
* **Standard Interview Questions:**
* Design a URL Shortener (TinyURL).
* Design a basic Pastebin.


* **Visuals:**

---

## Tier 2: The Intermediate Level

**Focus:** Availability, Reliability, and Data Consistency.

* **Core Concepts:**
* **CAP Theorem:** Consistency, Availability, and Partition Tolerance tradeoffs.
* **Database Deep-Dive:** Indexing, Sharding (Partitioning), and Replication (Master-Slave).
* **Asynchronous Processing:** Message Queues (Kafka, RabbitMQ) and Pub/Sub models.
* **Microservices:** API Gateways, Service Discovery, and Circuit Breakers.


* **Caching Strategies:** Write-through, Write-around, and Eviction policies (LRU/LFU).
* **Standard Interview Questions:**
* Design a Notification System.
* Design a Rate Limiter (Token Bucket vs. Leaky Bucket).
* Design a Top-K Trending Topics system (using Redis/Storm).


* **Visuals:**

---

## Tier 3: The Advanced Level

**Focus:** Global Scalability, Fault Tolerance, and Observability.

* **Core Concepts:**
* **Distributed Consensus:** Paxos or Raft algorithms (How databases stay in sync).
* **Gossip Protocols:** How nodes in a large cluster talk to each other.
* **Data Partitioning:** Consistent Hashing (The backbone of DynamoDB/Cassandra).
* **Monitoring & Logging:** ELK Stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, Kibana) and Distributed Tracing.
* **Security:** OAuth2, JWT, and Rate Limiting at the edge.


* **Standard Interview Questions:**
* Design a Video Streaming Service (Netflix/YouTube - Handling petabytes of data).
* Design a Distributed File System (GCS/S3).
* Design a Real-time Messenger (WhatsApp/Slack - Handling millions of concurrent socket connections).
